Research Interests

I am a researcher interested in questions in cosmology and gravitational physics. I enjoy using computational and analytical methods to solve the questions. I have worked on research projects related to generalized dark matter and neutron-star equations of state. I am also interested in the cosmological perturbation theory and cosmic microwave background.

Research Experience

Influence of Data Noise on Spectral Representation in the Relativistic Inverse Stellar Structure Problem | PI: Dr. Lee Lindblom

University of California, San Diego | Jul 2024 - Present

Numerically solved the Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass and radius data from a specified state equation. Utilized the Levenberg–Marquardt algorithm to determine optimal Chebyshev-based spectral parameters and central pressures based on the generated data. Introduced noise to analyze modeling errors.

Model Independent Probes of Dark Sector Physics | PI: Prof. Daniel Grin

Haverford College | May 2023 - Present

Developed a method to constrain dark matter models using principal component analysis and the generalized dark matter (GDM) framework. Utilized the CLASS code for cosmological modeling.
